Badminton is a racquet sport played using racquets to hit a shuttlecock across a net. The most common forms are singles (one player per side) and doubles (two players per side). While it can be played casually outdoors, professional games take place on a rectangular indoor court.

History and Origins

The sport's origins trace back centuries to the game of battledore and shuttlecock, played in ancient Greece, China, and India. The modern game developed in British India in the mid-19th century, evolving from "poona," a variation played by British army officers. It received its name from Badminton House in Gloucestershire, England

Gameplay and Scoring

Points are scored by hitting the shuttlecock and landing it within the opposing team's court. Each side can only strike the shuttlecock once before it crosses the net. A match is typically best-of-three games, each to 21 points, with a two-point advantage needed to win.

Equipment

The essential equipment includes lightweight rackets (made of various materials) and a shuttlecock (a feathered or plastic projectile). The shuttlecock's unique flight characteristics are a defining feature of the sport.

Badminton in the Olympics

Badminton debuted as a demonstration sport in 1972 and became an official Olympic sport in 1992. The mixed doubles event was added in 1996.
